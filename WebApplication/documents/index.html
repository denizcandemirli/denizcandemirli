PROCESS OF THE PROJECT


Project development between the months from October '23 to February '24.


  • October '23

    Our Project Beginnings

    This year's ITBE Fusion Lab focuses on revolutionizing infrastructure with the construction of the new Donnersberger Bridge. We're utilizing cutting-edge technology such as BIM Modeling with IFC import/export, GIS Implementation for CityGML generation from derived data, and developing user-friendly software like a web map client and an IFC model viewer for seamless accessibility on web pages. Join us as we pave the way for the future of bridge construction and urban development.

  • November '23

    Creating Design Concepts & Working on GIS Data

    In the exploration phase of our FUSION LAB project, we immerse ourselves in the intricate tapestry of GIS data, extracting invaluable insights to inform our creative process. Harnessing the power of this data, we sculpt concept designs for the upcoming Donnersberger Bridge, infusing it with avant-garde technology and visionary urban planning principles. Through meticulous analysis and bold imagination, we lay the foundation for a bridge that transcends expectations, seamlessly blending functionality with artistic innovation.

  • December '23

    Adaption of Lane Model to 3D & Creating the BIM of the Bridge

    After meticulous deliberation, we finalize the design for the Donnersberger Bridge, incorporating insights from our GIS data analysis and community feedback. With the design confirmed, we embark on creating a comprehensive BIM model, integrating every detail with precision. Simultaneously, we utilize ArcGIS Pro to extract the existing bridge's shapefile data, seamlessly transferring it to FME Workbench for the meticulous creation of the CityGML representation, ensuring accuracy and fidelity to the real-world structure.

  • January '24

    CityGML and IFC Classes

    We employ Revit BIM software to meticulously craft the IFC classes, ensuring every element of the new bridge is accurately represented in the digital model. Concurrently, we utilize FME Software to generate CityGML data by exporting the shapefile derived from the georeferenced IFC model of the new bridge in ArcGIS Pro, ensuring seamless integration of spatial and building information.

  • February '24

    Software Development

    In the final stage of our FUSION LAB project, we embark on the development of a dynamic website dedicated to showcasing our progress and findings. Central to this endeavor is the integration of an IFC web viewer, allowing stakeholders to interact with the intricate details of the bridge design in real-time. Through seamless web-based access to the IFC model, we aim to foster greater transparency and engagement, empowering users to explore the project's evolution with ease.

Creating IFC

Architectural Informatics

In creating the IFC (Industry Foundation Classes) file of the Donnersberger Bridge within Revit, we meticulously imported and integrated various architectural, structural, and MEP models. Ensuring precise detailing of structural elements, materials, and connections, we meticulously modeled every aspect of the bridge within the Revit environment. Subsequently, I executed the seamless export of the comprehensive model to the IFC format, thereby preserving vital BIM (Building Information Modeling) data essential for streamlined collaboration and interoperability across diverse software platforms and project stakeholders.

  • Task: Creating Ifc File of the Bridge
  • Category: Architectural

GIS

CityGML

In leveraging FME software for GIS implementations, we spearheaded the creation of a CityGML file for the Donnersberger Bridge. By meticulously configuring FME workflows, I seamlessly integrated diverse geospatial datasets, including architectural, topographical, and infrastructural information pertinent to the bridge. Employing FME's robust capabilities, I ensured the accurate representation of the bridge's attributes, such as geometry, semantics, and metadata, within the CityGML format. This comprehensive CityGML file serves as a valuable asset for GIS applications, facilitating efficient analysis, visualization, and decision-making processes for urban planning and infrastructure management.

  • Task: Creating Web Map Client
  • Category: Geospatial Information Science
