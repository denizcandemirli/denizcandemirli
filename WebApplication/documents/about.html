About Project

ITBE Fusion Lab 23/24

Introduction:

The ITBE Fusion Lab 2023/24 is a collaborative effort among students from diverse backgrounds aimed at tackling real-world challenges in the built environment using design and software development skills. The project encompasses two core components: a design task focusing on the Donnersberger Bridge and a software development task to facilitate public participation in the design process.

Design Task:

The design task revolves around the importance of the Donnersberger Bridge for various transportation modes, proposing replacement bridge designs, and ensuring traffic management during renovation/replacement. The objectives include creating an iconic landmark for Munich while addressing sustainability and cost-efficiency aspects.

Key Objectives:

1- Investigate the significance of the Donnersberger Bridge for different transportation modes.

2- Develop replacement bridge designs considering traffic management during renovation/replacement.

3- Propose an iconic design ensuring sustainability and cost efficiency.

4- Ensure sufficient class assignment, material properties, and geometric details in bridge models.

5- Explore "exciting" bridge construction methods.

Approach:

Analyze existing data on the site and bridge importance.

Develop replacement bridge designs using innovative construction methods.

Implement traffic management strategies during renovation/replacement.

Incorporate sustainability and cost-efficiency considerations.

Utilize BIM models with LOD 200 and CityGML datasets with LOD 2.

Software Task:

The software task aims to develop a web-based system enabling public participation in the design process. It includes a frontend for 3D/2D views of the bridge and surroundings, a server-side database for design models and comments, and integration of third-party sources like CityGML and traffic data.

Key Objectives:

1- Develop a web-based system for public participation in the bridge design process.

2- Provide 3D/2D views of the bridge and surroundings.

3- Enable users to leave comments and feedback.

4- Integrate third-party sources like CityGML and traffic data.

5- Support BIM modeling application with IFC export functionalities.

6- Integration of Ifc Web Viewer.

Approach:

Utilize open-source libraries like IFC.js, XBIM WebGui, or Autodesk Platform Services.

Develop a user-friendly frontend with 3D/2D views and comment functionality.

Implement a robust server-side database for storing design models and comments.

Integrate third-party sources for enriched data.

Ensure compatibility with BIM modeling applications and traffic simulators.

Conclusion:

The ITBE Fusion Lab 2023/24 project presents an exciting opportunity for interdisciplinary collaboration in addressing real-world challenges in the built environment. By combining innovative bridge design with advanced software development, the project aims to create sustainable, iconic solutions while actively engaging the public in the design process. Through careful planning and execution, we aim to deliver impactful outcomes that contribute positively to the city district of Munich and its future mobility needs.
